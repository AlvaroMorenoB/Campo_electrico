Your source for diverse and challenging problems in physics.

Capacitor Problems

  • Problem 1: A parallel plate capacitor has a separation of d = 0.005 m and a potential difference of ΔV = 200 V.
    Calculate the electric field E between the plates.
    Bonus: What ΔV is required to double the field, keeping the distance constant?
  • Problem 2: A capacitor with plates separated by 0.01 m has a charge of q = 3 × 10-6 C.
    Calculate the force F acting on the charge if the electric field is E = 3 × 104 N/C.
    Then, find the acceleration a for a particle with mass m = 0.005 kg.

Electric Field Problems

  • Problem 1: A point charge of q = 2 × 10-6 C is placed in a uniform electric field E = 5 × 103 N/C.
    Calculate the force F on the charge and the work done to move it 0.02 m in the direction of the field.
  • Problem 2: The potential difference between two points in a uniform field is ΔV = 500 V.
    The distance between the points is d = 0.01 m. Calculate the electric field E.
